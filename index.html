What is a hackathon?

Here's what that fancy word means!

We saved you the googling

“A hackathon, a hacker neologism, is an event when programmers meet to do collaborative computer programming.” -- Wikipedia
Hackathons provide a venue for self-expression and creativity through technology. People with technical backgrounds come together, form teams around a problem or idea, and collaboratively code a unique solution from scratch — these generally take shape in the form of websites, mobile apps, and robots.

A notable example of a hackathon “hack,” GroupMe is a group messaging app that was acquired by Skype for over $50 million. Other notable examples include the Facebook “Like” button and Facebook Chat which were both first demoed at internal company hackathons.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to stay the whole 24 hours?

Although you don’t have to, we recommend you do. This gives you the most amount of time to work on your project. But if you do leave early, we require you to come back in the morning the next day to proceed with the competition.

Will food be provided?

Yes – dinner, snacks, and breakfast in the morning will be provided free of cost. You can bring your own food if you like as well.

What if I’m really tired in the middle of the night?

We have made sleeping arrangements. If you wish to, you may take a nap or sleep through the night.

Do I need a laptop?

Laptops or any other devices will not be provided at the venue. If you require laptop(s) for your project, you are expected to bring them with you along with chargers. The same goes for any other software/hardware as well.

Will there be WiFi?

You do not have to bring an internet dongle or any other such device, unless you require very fast internet speeds. Free and moderately fast internet will be provided at the site of the hackathon.

Can I work on my project before the hackathon starts?

NO. Working on a project beforehand will result in automatic disqualification. All work must be completed during the course of the hackathon. You will be monitored from time to time and must regularly upload your code (if applicable) to GitHub.

Do I need to know how to use GitHub?

We recommend that you learn the basics of git and GitHub and have git installed on at least one laptop in your team. An introduction to git/GitHub is available here.

Prerequisites & Preparation

Don't be afraid! If you are a beginner in the 6-8th grade category, you could use MIT's App Inventor to make an Android app - quick and easy. Other GUI based platforms such as Scratch are also permitted in the 6-8th grade category. You could also learn HTML, CSS and JavaScript in a very short period of time!

Arduino and Raspberry Pi projects are also welcome. Basic materials will be provided at the venue, but you will have to get any specific components.

Of course, you can also use any of these programming languages if you are experienced.
